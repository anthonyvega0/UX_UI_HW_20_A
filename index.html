Laguna Food Pantry

LFP is a local organization that collects and distributes food to people in need. They offer free, fresh groceries five mornings a week to anyone who signs in at their front desk to shop in a retail-style setting.

Task: Redesign A Non Profit Organization's website

TOOLS: Figma, Invision, Miro, Draw io

Competitor Analysis

By using a parent brand for the competitor analysis we quickly realized the importance of keeping certain aspects such as color aligned with their brand. This would allow Laguna Food Pantry to keep certain strengths such as brand awareness.
We noticed when looking at competitors, some had multiple ctas directed at different types of donors. We thought this was very effective and decided to build a homepage that would be easy to navigate for multiple different types of users.

Problem Statement

The current website's navigation contained unnecessary links that led to the same page - confusing and frustrating the user. This paired with the fact that there was a lack of user specific cta's made for a tedious userflow for someone looking to donate.

Conclussion

Keeping a consistent brand identity.

It was a challenge to unify the content, structure, and UI elements under one brand vision. The key was to solidify the brand words in the beginning and use those words to influence every design decision.

Communicating information effectively.

The content structure can make or break a user experience. I experimented with different variations of content chunking to maximize comprehension and enable users to scan the page quickly.

Tackling responsive web design.

Knowing that users are becoming increasingly more mobile made this project a valuable exercise in responsive web design. We as user experience designers similarly need to be responsive to the expectation that digital content should be accessible across a growing number of screen sizes, platforms, and orientations.
